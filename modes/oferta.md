# Mode: oferta (single job evaluation)

Evaluate a single job description against the candidate's CV and profile. Produce
a structured report with the 6 blocks below.

## Block 1 — Role summary (3-5 sentences)

What is this role actually about? Strip the marketing copy. What will the
person DO day-to-day? What team are they on? What problems are they solving?
If the JD is vague, say so and infer what you can from company context.

## Block 2 — CV match

- **Strengths** — 3-6 bullets. Specific overlaps between the CV and the JD.
  Reference concrete experience, not generic claims.
- **Gaps** — 2-5 bullets. Honest. If the candidate is missing something
  important, say so. Distinguish between "missing but learnable" and
  "missing and probably disqualifying".
- **Match score** — 1-5 on CV-fit alone (this is ONE input to overall_score).

## Block 3 — Level strategy

Is the candidate a stretch, a fit, or over-qualified for this level? How should
they pitch themselves in the application + interview? If it's a stretch, what
evidence from the CV closes the gap? If it's over-qualified, how do they avoid
being screened out for being too senior?

## Block 4 — Compensation research

Expected base + bonus + equity range for this role, at this company, at this
level, in this location. Be specific: give a range, not a point estimate. Cite
your reasoning (levels.fyi benchmarks, company stage, city cost-of-living
adjustments, public comp bands if mentioned in the JD).

If you don't know the company's comp ladder, say so and give a market-rate
range for the level + location instead.

## Block 5 — Personalization hooks

3 bullet points the candidate can use in a cover letter, LinkedIn outreach, or
recruiter call. Each hook should tie something specific from the CV to
something specific in the JD / company's current focus. Generic ("I'm
passionate about AI") is useless — be specific.

## Block 6 — Interview prep

- **STAR+R stories to prepare** — 3-5 stories from the CV that would answer
  likely behavioral questions for THIS role. Name the story and the skill
  it demonstrates.
- **Likely technical / role-specific questions** — 4-6 questions that are
  high-probability for this archetype + company.

## Final recommendation

One of:
- **apply** — score ≥ 4.0, no deal-breakers
- **maybe** — score 3.5-3.9, or score ≥ 4.0 with a flagged concern the
  candidate should think about
- **skip** — score < 3.5, or any hard filter failed (comp, location,
  work auth, level mismatch too severe)

Explain the recommendation in 2-3 sentences. Be direct.
